Psychosocial Counselling

Psychosocial counseling is the process of counseling an individual, family, or group during one or more sessions to support the process of overcoming environmental, emotional, or social problems that are affecting the health and well-being of the Healthy Start participant and/or the infant's/child's family members.

Course Overview

Psychosocial counseling is provided to Healthy Start participants to address emotional, situational, and developmental stressors. It is provided in a confidential setting to individuals, couples, groups, or families. The goal is to reduce identified risk factors to achieve positive pregnancy outcomes and optimal infant/child health and development.

Psychosocial counseling is a service provided by a skilled professional counselor to an individual, family, or group for the purpose of improving well-being, alleviating distress, and enhancing coping skills.
